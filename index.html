Subsea Engineering

Introduction

Subsea engineering is a specialised branch of offshore engineering that focuses on designing, installing, and maintaining equipment and infrastructure beneath the oceanâ€™s surface. It plays a crucial role in offshore energy production, especially in the oil and gas industry, where engineers work with systems placed thousands of meters underwater.

Subsea engineers must understand advanced technologies such as remotely operated vehicles (ROVs), dynamic positioning, and high-pressure equipment that can survive extreme underwater conditions. The field also increasingly supports renewable energy, including offshore wind and tidal systems. Subsea engineering is challenging but rewarding, combining innovation, problem-solving, and global-scale environmental responsibility.
